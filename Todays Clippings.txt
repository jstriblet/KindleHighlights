Thank You for Arguing What Aristotle, Lincoln, and Homer Simpson Can Teach Us About the Art of Persuasion  
- Your Highlight on page 65-65 | Added on Tuesday, 14 June 2016 22:28:31

Rhetoric is an agnostic art; it requires more adaptation than righteousness. You adapt to the values of your audience. Values take on a different meaning in rhetoric as well. Rhetorical values do not necessarily represent rightness or truth; they merely constitute what people valuehonor, faith, steadfastness, money, toys. Support your audiences values, and you earn the temporary trustworthiness that rhetoric calls virtue. Among the ways to pump up your rhetorical virtue, we covered four: Brag. Get a witness to brag for you. Reveal a tactical flaw. Switch sides when the powers that be do. A variation is the Eddie Haskell Ploy, which throws your support behind the inevitable. When you know you will lose, preempt your opponent by taking his side
